The Industry Problem

The Advisory Model Was Built
for a World That No Longer Exists

The old model separated portfolio management from legal planning — and both from risk engineering. That fragmentation worked when markets were predictable. They aren't anymore.

The Legacy Model

Siloed, Reactive, Correlation-Dependent

Most advisory practices still run on a portfolio-only thesis. Allocation models that assume negative stock-bond correlation. Estate plans drafted without knowledge of account structures. Tax strategies that live in spreadsheets, disconnected from legal reality.

When markets moved predictably, this worked. In a regime defined by fiscal dominance, sticky inflation, and broken correlations — it doesn't.

The Integrated Model

Engineered, Coordinated, Structurally Resilient

Risk engineering starts with mapping structural exposure — counterparty risk, correlation breakdown, fiat currency erosion — and designing around it. Not predicting. Designing.

When that architecture is built in coordination with estate planning attorneys who understand the financial thesis, every trust provision, beneficiary designation, and tax structure works in concert. That's the gap we close.
